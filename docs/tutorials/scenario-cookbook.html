A · Market foundations

Base market climate_solutions_basic_linear.json

The minimal scenario: a fixed cap, an explicit auction, and a few firms on linear MAC curves. The price clears where total demand meets auction supply.

Key knobs: total_cap, auction_offered, per-firm initial_emissions / max_abatement / cost_slope.
python ets_framework.py --config examples/climate_solutions_basic_linear.json
Look for
  • Equilibrium Carbon Price — the single clearing price.
  • Per-firm Abatement vs Allowance Buys: flatter MAC (lower cost_slope) abates more.

Banking & borrowing climate_solutions_banking_borrowing.json

Let firms carry allowances across years. With a rising expected price, firms bank now to use later; borrowing pulls future allowances forward.

Per year: banking_allowed, borrowing_allowed, borrowing_limit, expectation_rule.
python ets_framework.py --config examples/climate_solutions_banking_borrowing.json
Look for
  • Total Banked / Borrowed Allowances and the Ending Bank Balance per firm.
  • Banking smooths the price path across years versus a no-banking run.

Auction controls climate_solutions_auction_controls.json

Add a reserve price and a minimum bid-coverage rule. Below the reserve, allowances go unsold; below the coverage floor, the auction is cancelled.

Per year: auction_reserve_price, minimum_bid_coverage, unsold_treatment (reserve / cancel / carry_forward).
python ets_framework.py --config examples/climate_solutions_auction_controls.json
Look for
  • Unsold Allowances, Auction Coverage Ratio, and where the price sticks at the reserve.

B · Abatement modelling

Piecewise MAC + no-regret blocks climate_solutions_mac_pathway.json

Model abatement as discrete cost blocks (efficiency → fuel switch → CCS). A block is taken whole when the price clears its marginal cost. Costs may be negative — "no-regret" measures undertaken at any price.

"abatement_type": "piecewise",
"mac_blocks": [
  {"amount": 5.0, "marginal_cost": -40.0},   // no-regret: net-saving
  {"amount": 8.0, "marginal_cost":  35.0},
  {"amount": 6.0, "marginal_cost": 120.0}
]
Rules: amount ≥ 0; marginal_cost may be negative; blocks sorted by non-decreasing cost.
Look for
  • Discrete jumps in Abatement as the price crosses each block's marginal_cost.
  • Negative-cost blocks are abated even when the price is ~0 (see the K-ETS Outlook recipe for a full negative-cost MACC).

Threshold abatement climate_solutions_threshold_pathway.json

The simplest non-linear model: a firm abates a fixed maximum once the price clears a single switching cost, nothing below it.

Per firm: abatement_type: "threshold", threshold_cost, max_abatement.
python ets_framework.py --config examples/climate_solutions_threshold_pathway.json
Look for
  • A step in abatement exactly at threshold_cost; flat on either side.

Technology switching climate_solutions_technology_switching.json

Give a firm several technologies (each its own MAC, fixed cost, and activity cap). The model picks the least-cost mix at the prevailing price via SLSQP.

Per firm: technology_options[] with fixed_cost, max_activity_share, and a MAC per option.
python ets_framework.py --config examples/climate_solutions_technology_switching.json
Look for
  • Chosen Technology / Technology Mix per firm, and how the mix shifts as the price rises.

C · Allocation & trade

Output-based allocation (OBA) k_ets_oba_benchmark.json

Allocate free permits by output × benchmark intensity rather than a flat ratio — the EU/K-ETS benchmarking approach. Cleaner firms come out long.

"production_output": 10.0,            // Mt product/yr
"benchmark_emission_intensity": 1.4   // tCO2/unit  →  free alloc = 14 Mt
When both are > 0, OBA free allocation overrides free_allocation_ratio.
Look for
  • Free Allocation per firm = intensity × output; firms below benchmark are net sellers.

Free-allocation phase-out climate_solutions_advanced_cbam_features.json

Taper free allocation to zero over time without editing every year by hand — a scenario-level trajectory per participant.

"free_allocation_trajectories": [
  {"participant_name": "Steel", "start_year": "2026", "end_year": "2034",
   "start_ratio": 0.6, "end_ratio": 0.0}
]
Look for
  • The firm's Free Allocation declining linearly year on year; rising allowance purchases.

CBAM exposure climate_solutions_cbam_exposure.json

Layer a Carbon Border Adjustment on exports: when a reference (e.g. EU) price exceeds the domestic price, exporters owe the gap on their embedded emissions.

"eua_price": 90.0,                 // year-level reference price
"cbam_export_share": 0.4,          // participant: 40% of activity exported
"cbam_coverage_ratio": 1.0
Look for
  • CBAM Gap (reference − domestic), Total CBAM Liability, and the revenue tracker (Domestic Retained vs CBAM Foregone).

Multi-jurisdiction CBAM k_ets_multi_jurisdiction_cbam.json

Different export destinations with different reference prices and coverage — liability is computed per jurisdiction and summed.

"cbam_jurisdictions": [
  {"name": "EU", "export_share": 0.3, "coverage_ratio": 1.0, "reference_price": 90.0},
  {"name": "UK", "export_share": 0.1, "coverage_ratio": 0.8, "reference_price": 75.0}
]
Look for
  • Per-jurisdiction CBAM Liability (EU) / (UK) columns and their total.

Sector caps k_ets_subsector_decomposition.json

Define caps and auction shares per sector; the total cap and free pool are derived from the sectors, and each firm draws free allocation from its sector's pool.

"sectors": [
  {"name": "Power",  "cap_trajectory": {"start_year":"2026","end_year":"2030","start_value":200,"end_value":150},
   "auction_share_trajectory": {"start_year":"2026","end_year":"2030","start_value":0.9,"end_value":1.0}}
],
// on each participant:  "sector_group": "Power", "sector_allocation_share": 0.5
Look for
  • Per-sector aggregates in the summary ({Sector} Total Abatement, cost percentiles).

D · Price formation

Hotelling banking — set model_approach

Treat allowances as an exhaustible resource: the price path rises at the discount rate so a fixed cumulative budget is exhausted optimally. Add a risk premium to steepen it. Set the approach on any scenario.

"model_approach": "hotelling",
"discount_rate": 0.055,
"risk_premium": 0.0,
// each year needs a "carbon_budget" to bisect on
Price path: P*(t) = λ·(1 + r + ρ)^(t−t₀), with λ set so cumulative residual emissions = the budget. The bundled climate_solutions_k_ets_outlook.json runs in Hotelling mode.
Look for
  • A smoothly rising price; total residual emissions matching the cumulative carbon_budget.

Nash–Cournot (market power) — set model_approach

Let named large players act strategically — withholding abatement to move the price — instead of as price-takers. Best-response iteration to a Nash equilibrium.

"model_approach": "nash_cournot",
"nash_strategic_participants": ["Power"]
Look for
  • The strategic firm abates less and the price differs from the competitive run; a convergence warning if the iteration can't settle.

Compare all approaches — set model_approach

Run competitive, Hotelling, and Nash side by side on one market to see how the price-formation assumption alone changes the outcome. Set "all" on a scenario; the runner emits one result set per approach.

"model_approach": "all"
For comparing different policy designs (rather than price-formation methods), put several scenarios in one file — see climate_solutions_compare_suite.json.
Look for
  • Three result sets (scenario name suffixed [Competitive] / [Hotelling] / [Nash-Cournot]) to compare prices.

E · Supply-side policy

Market Stability Reserve climate_solutions_msr_stability.json

A supply valve: withhold auction volume into a reserve when the banked surplus is too high; release it when the bank runs short.

"msr_enabled": true,
"msr_upper_threshold": 60.0, "msr_lower_threshold": 10.0,
"msr_withhold_rate": 0.15, "msr_release_rate": 20.0
Full reference: docs/multi-year-simulation.md.
Look for
  • MSR Withheld / Released / Reserve Pool columns; a steadier price than the MSR-off twin.

Carbon Cap Rule (adaptive cap) benmir_ccr_carbon_cap_rule.json

A Taylor-rule cap (Benmir, Roman & Taschini 2025): nudge each year's permit quantity by the prior year's emissions and abatement-cost gaps. Cuts price volatility ~27% in the example.

"ccr_enabled": true,
"ccr_phi_emissions": -30.0,        // tighten when emissions overshoot
"ccr_phi_abatement_cost": 15.0,    // loosen when abatement costs run hot
"ccr_reference_emissions": 424.0,
"ccr_reference_abatement_cost": 2600.0
Formula & theory: docs/carbon-cap-rule.md.
Look for
  • CCR Cap Adjustment / Cost Deviation / Emissions Deviation; the held-down post-shock price peak vs the fixed-cap twin.

F · Economic feedback

Feedback A · price-elastic baseline feedback_a_price_elastic_baseline.json

Let carbon-intensive activity contract as the price rises — "demand destruction" solved inside clearing. The same tightening cap then produces a far flatter price path.

"reference_carbon_price": 55.0,        // scenario anchor P_ref
// per participant:
"output_price_elasticity": 0.5         // ε ≥ 0; baseline = E0·max(0, 1 − ε·(P−P_ref)/P_ref)
Reference: docs/feedback-price-elastic-baseline.md.
Look for
  • A flatter, lower price path than the inelastic twin; the per-firm Initial Emissions column now shows the price-scaled baseline (activity contracting).
  • Set output_price_elasticity or reference_carbon_price to 0 to recover the inelastic model exactly.

Feedback B · external-model coupling feedback_b_coupling_demo.py python

Iterate the ETS to a joint equilibrium with an external energy / CGE / DSGE model through a pluggable adapter — general-equilibrium feedback without embedding a GE model.

from ets.coupling import run_coupled_simulation, ElasticityExternalModel

result = run_coupled_simulation(
    config,
    ElasticityExternalModel(elasticity=0.5, reference_price=55.0),
    max_iterations=40, tolerance=0.25, relaxation=0.5)   # relaxation damps oscillation
print(result.converged, result.iterations)
Run the demo: python examples/feedback_b_coupling_demo.py · adapter contract: docs/feedback-coupling.md.
Look for
  • result.price_history converging (e.g. 171 → 51 → 89 → 100); converged in ~4 iterations.
  • Swap ElasticityExternalModel for your own class with a respond() method to plug in a real model.

G · Real-world & analysis

K-ETS Outlook (full real-world model) climate_solutions_k_ets_outlook.json

A standalone 3-scenario (Base / Middle / Ideal) Korea-ETS configuration — 25 MACC technologies across 6 sectors, Hotelling banking, learning-curve cost decay, and negative-cost no-regret measures. Prices are in ₩/tCO₂.

python ets_framework.py --config examples/climate_solutions_k_ets_outlook.json
Generated from a K-ETS model via examples/build_k_ets_outlook.py (needs the source model output); the JSON itself is fully runnable.
Look for
  • Three scenarios with diverging price paths; sectors abating their negative-cost blocks first.
  • Prices in the tens of thousands — that's ₩/tCO₂, not a bug.

KAU–EUA convergence k_ets_kau_eua_convergence.json

Ramp a domestic price floor toward an EU price over time to study convergence and the auction-revenue / CBAM-liability trade-off.

"price_floor_trajectory": {"start_year":"2026","end_year":"2035","start_value":18,"end_value":85}
Look for
  • The price floor rising year on year; Potential Revenue if KAU=EUA vs CBAM Foregone Revenue.

Calibration k_ets_calibration_request.json api

Fit each firm's linear MAC slope so the model reproduces an observed price series (Nelder–Mead).

POST /api/calibrate     # body: config + observed_prices + participant_names
This is an API payload, not a --config run. Methodology: docs/analysis-tools.md.
Look for
  • The fitted cost_slope per participant and the residual fit error in the response.

Batch sweep k_ets_batch_eua_sweep.json api

Run a Cartesian product of parameter values (e.g. several EUA reference prices × caps) for sensitivity analysis.

POST /api/batch-run     # body: { config, sweeps: [ {path, values}, … ] }
API payload. Methodology: docs/analysis-tools.md.
Look for
  • One result row per parameter combination — scan the price/abatement response across the grid.
